About Me

Hello! I'm Adam Omarali.

My mission is to make the world a better place. Solving malnutrition, diseases and lack of access to resources are all on my radar! In general, I like solving problems, especially the ones no one else will.

I love breaking down systems and humans to see how they operate. Learning and expanding on past philosophies, thinking critically about data to form knowledge, and applying that knowledge to form wisdom.

On the side, I perform magic, think about big questions, make music and meet like-minded people (ps. that means you should reach out to me).

Fun Fact: In 2015, I was ranked #1 in North America for the Pokemon Trading Card Game, in the Junior Division. Why did I do it: because there were scholarships.

My Values

Understand my heart: what I live and what determines by actions.

1

Be Intentional with Your Time and Actions

The opposite of not wasting time is gaining insane value during that time. The best way to do that and avoid stupid decisions is to be intentional.

2

Diversity In Experiences and Learning

Growing your T is so important and will help you become more successful, coming up with crazy ideas that no one could have thought of.

3

Olympic Level Performance for What's High Value

This could also be classified as high standards...what you produce should be Olympic level, because your training to become an Olympic level CEO. And how you train is how you perform. Giving 110% at everything will cause burnout.

4

Make Magic

Don't confine yourself to the status quo. A random person should hear what your thinking and believe its magic. The only way the world drastically changes is if we can be innovative and think outside of the box.

5

Look in the Mirror and see your Tomorrow Self

Being authentic is so key, it's what makes you interesting. And by looking at your tomorrow self, you are visualizing your future.

6

Less Complaints and Worrying, and More Doing

Don't waste your life fussing about went wrong because you're lowering your chances of doing something right. It's a loop you never want to fall into, think of a tornado, once you're in the circle, you're done.
